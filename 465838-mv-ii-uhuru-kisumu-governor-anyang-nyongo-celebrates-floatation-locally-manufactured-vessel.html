MV II Uhuru: Kisumu Governor Anyang' Nyong'o Celebrates Floatation of Locally Manufactured Vessel

  • President Uhuru Kenyatta launched a series of projects in the Nyanza region, including the floatation of MV Uhuru II at Kisumu Port
  • The 100-meter vessel has a capacity of carrying up to 22 wagons and an estimated capacity of two million litres of crude oil per trip
  • The vessel will now operate in the region with MV Uhuru 1, which was launched in mid-2021

President Uhuru Kenyatta has flagged off the floatation of MV Uhuru II at the Kisumu port.

The repairing and refitting of MV Uhuru 1 started in 2019 by Kenya Railways Authority in conjunction with the KDF.

Uhuru welcomed the operationalisation of Kisumu Shipyard, saying the facility will create business and job opportunities for thousands of young people in the country.

He pointed out that the Kisumu Shipyard will also improve transport and safety in the lake, enhance intermodal transport as well as boost fishing, tourism and other economic activities in the Eastern Africa region.

With the successful construction of the MV Uhuru II ship, which will be commissioned later this year at the Kisumu Shipyard, Uhuru expressed satisfaction that Kenya has become a pioneer shipbuilding nation in Africa.

During the foundation laying exercise of MV Uhuru II, the president also officiated the flagging off of the refurbished MV Uhuru ferry that had stalled at the Kisumu port for more than a decade.

Since February 2021, MV Uhuru has been ferrying petroleum commodities to Uganda when its renovation was complete.

Since its revival, the cargo ferry has transported more than 50 million litres of petroleum products to Jinja in Uganda, from where other regions in the great lakes including DRC and Burundi and Rwanda, have been sourcing their fuel.
