A Wildlife Sanctuary in Verona

Parco Natura Viva is a remarkable wildlife sanctuary situated in the beautiful city of Verona, Italy. It spans over 240,000 square meters and provides a natural habitat for a diverse range of animal species.

Home to Over 1,500 Animals

Parco Natura Viva is home to more than 1,500 animals, representing over 250 different species from around the world. From endangered species to exotic creatures, visitors can observe and learn about the fascinating wildlife that resides within the park.

A Conservation and Breeding Program

One of the park’s primary objectives is the conservation and protection of endangered species. Parco Natura Viva actively participates in breeding programs to help safeguard at-risk animals, contributing to the preservation of biodiversity.

Interactive Experiences

Parco Natura Viva offers interactive experiences that allow visitors to get up close and personal with the animals. From feeding sessions to guided tours, visitors can learn about different species and their conservation needs while enjoying unforgettable encounters.

The Dinosaur Park

Parco Natura Viva is not only home to living animals but also features a thrilling Dinosaur Park. Visitors can embark on a journey through time and explore life-size replicas of these ancient reptiles, bringing the prehistoric world to life.

Education and Awareness

Education and awareness are fundamental aspects of Parco Natura Viva’s mission. The park offers educational programs and workshops for visitors of all ages, aiming to promote a deeper understanding of wildlife conservation and inspire a sense of responsibility towards the environment.

Stunning Botanical Gardens

In addition to its remarkable wildlife, Parco Natura Viva boasts stunning botanical gardens. Visitors can wander through beautifully landscaped spaces, enjoying the abundance of plant species that complement the natural beauty of the park.

Eco-friendly Initiatives

Parco Natura Viva is committed to sustainable practices and eco-friendly initiatives. From recycling programs to renewable energy sources, the park strives to minimize its environmental impact and promote conservation principles.

FAQs

1. How long does a visit to Parco Natura Viva usually take?

A visit to Parco Natura Viva typically takes around 4-5 hours to explore both the Safari Park and the Fauna Park. However, you can spend more or less time depending on your interests and the activities you choose to participate in.

2. Are there any restrictions for interacting with the animals?

For the safety and well-being of the animals, there may be certain restrictions on interacting with them. Some animals may only be observed from a distance, while others can be interacted with under the supervision of trained guides. It is important to follow the guidelines provided by the park to ensure the welfare of the animals.

3. Can I bring my own food and drinks inside the park?

Yes, you are allowed to bring your own food and drinks inside the park. There are picnic areas and designated spots where you can enjoy your own meals. However, there are also restaurants and cafes available within the park if you prefer to purchase food on-site.

4. Is Parco Natura Viva accessible for individuals with disabilities?

Parco Natura Viva is committed to providing accessibility for all visitors. The park has paved pathways and ramps to ensure ease of movement for wheelchair users and individuals with disabilities. Additionally, there are accessible restrooms and designated parking spaces available.

5. Are there any discounts available for tickets?

Parco Natura Viva offers various discounts for children, students, seniors, and groups. It is advisable to check the official website or contact the park directly for information on current discounts and promotions.
