FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Paphos?

A: The best time to visit Paphos is during the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) months when the weather is pleasant and temperatures are milder.

Q: What are some must-visit landmarks in Paphos?

A: Some must-visit landmarks in Paphos include the Paphos Archaeological Park, the Tomb of the Kings, Paphos Castle, and the House of Dionysus.

Q: Are there any outdoor activities to do in Paphos?

A: Yes, Paphos offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking in the Akamas Peninsula, exploring the Avakas Gorge, and enjoying water sports along the coastline.

Q: What is the currency used in Paphos?

A: The currency used in Paphos, as well as the rest of Cyprus, is the Euro (€).

Q: How is the food in Paphos?

A: The food in Paphos is a delightful blend of traditional Cypriot cuisine with influences from Greek, Turkish, and Middle Eastern flavors. Don’t miss out on trying local specialties like souvlaki, halloumi cheese, and loukoumades.

Q: Is English widely spoken in Paphos?

A: Yes, English is widely spoken in Paphos, particularly in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. You will have no trouble communicating with locals during your visit.
