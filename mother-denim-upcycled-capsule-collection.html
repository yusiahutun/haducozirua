Mother Premieres Capsule Collection Made from Upcycled Materials

 Posted on June 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  491 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Mother has taken the step toward sustainability. This week, the denim label launched 60% Mother, a capsule collection that’s 60 percent overstock fabric (hence the name) from past seasons and 40 percent materials found at wholesale vintage recycling plants across Los Angeles.

“We are committed to only working with what is available at local rag houses as well as within our own inventory of excess fabric, damages, and overstock,” says Tim Kaeding, Mother’s cofounder and creative director, to BAZAAR.com. “Each garment in the 21-piece capsule is deconstructed and reimagined creating unique features and characteristics.”

Mother

60% Mother collection

For more than a decade, Kaeding and his partner, Lela Becker, have built Mother into a brand beloved by celebrities (Meghan Markle is a devotee) and fashion insiders. The label is noted for its ’70s-inspired styles—think flared silhouettes, tattered treatments, and fun embroideries—along with focusing on amazing fits for all sizes. But what hasn’t been top of mind, at least until now, is producing pieces that don’t add to landfill waste.

Environmentalism in fashion has slowly but surely gained traction over the last decade. Brands, both big and small, have made efforts to reduce their carbon footprints and create collections sourced from deadstock fabrics, particularly last year, when mills were shuttered due to the pandemic. Acne Studios, for example, created a line that repurposes materials from past collections. Miu Miu presented a collection from upcycled vintage dresses. And now, Mother is following suit with a selection of jumpsuits, skirts, dresses, and, of course, varying cuts of jeans that all center on patchwork.

Mother

60% Mother collection

“For us specifically, many of our pieces are constructed of pre- and post-consumer waste, so in order to make a full garment, we needed to use multiple pieces of fabric,” says Kaeding. “Our jumpsuit was created by making a quilt of discarded shrink blankets that would otherwise be considered waste.”

As a result, all the pieces offered in the capsule are 100 percent unique, with Kaeding adding that the collection “will evolve organically.” Moreover, the brand is taking all the lessons learned from creating 60% Mother and will steadily apply them to the company overall.

“We already have a good portion of our line using eco-friendly fabrics,” Kaeding says. “In addition to our current practices, this collection and future mainline collections will be packaged in biodegradable 100 percent recycled low-density polyethylene bags. While we know we are far from where we need to be, we are committed to learning and evolving.”
