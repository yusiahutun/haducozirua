Martin Short did everything he could for a laugh.

Leave it to the Comedy Central and Saturday Night Live alumnus to turn a first-ever awards show into a success.

The versatile comedian and actor showed off his amazing talents and many characters to host the inaugural Canadian Screen Awards in Toronto on Sunday night.

Ready for his close-up! That's Martin Short under that fat suit as Jiminy Glick, arriving to host the first-ever Canadian Screen Awards on Sunday

Short put on the fat suit to portray his famous character Jiminy Glick, who definitely livened things up in that moss-green suit and matching tie and lavender shirt.

The entertainer even donned a red Scottish kilt for a separate skit.

Short had the audience reeling when he kicked up his bare shins and leaped into the arms of a fellow Scotsman in green kilt.

Ladies rule: Actress Laura Vandervoort vamped it up in a sexy red gown, while Sandra Oh was elegant in her black and red sequin-striped frock

Men who wear plaid: Martin kicked up his shins - and his red kilt - as he leapt into the arms of a fellow Scot

Celebrate: Martin got his harness removed by two skimpily clad ladies in red two-piece outfits

The ceremony, which took place at Sony Centre, replaces both the Genie and Gemini award shows and recognises the best that film, television and digital media productions have to offer. 

'You’re there to celebrate an audience full of people that have worked hard and to celebrate all the great work that has been done the last year,' Short told Citynews.ca.

The Canadian native has proved himself worthy in both comedy and drama in the US, but he's never lost his love for the place where he came from.

Fashion statement: Erica Durance sizzled in her black hourglass gown with peek shoulder, while Natalie Lisinska vamped it up in her bare-backed black number

They wore it well: Alice Morel-Michaud shimmered in a black sequin striped frock, while Catherine O'Hara was elegantly draped in formal black

Joining Short at the event was actress Laura Vandervoort, who was simply stunning in a strapless red floor-length gown, and actress Sandra Oh, who looked glamorous in a black long-sleeved number with shimmering red stripes.

Sandra, who wore her long hair in a cascade of curls around her shoulders, matched her dress with a pair of simple black heels.

Saving Hope nominee Erica Durance was a vision in a black formal gown with front bow and peek shoulders.

Rocker chic: Emily Haines walked on the wild side in her black mini and black leather and fringe jacket

Fun times: Martin seemed to have a ball hosting the show and he got to drink champagne

Hands on: Dragon's Den stars Kevin O'Leary, Mike Armitage, Arlene Dickinson, Lisa Gabrielle and Bruce Croxon posed with their award for best reality series

Erica wore her hair in a loose side chignon that showed off her dangling drop earrings.

Actress Catherine O'Hara may have borrowed a look from the elegant 1940s by wearing a slinky black long-sleeved gown with trailing hemline.

Singer-songwriter Emily Haines was rocker chic in a black dress with scissor hemline and a black leather jacket with gold fringe, along with black ankle boots.

Wow moment: James Cromwell won a best actor award for Still Mine

Les Pee-Wee 3D nominee Alice Morel-Michaud looked pretty in a sequin and sheer black frock and peek-toe heels. 

Actress and fellow nominee Natalie Lisinska was channeling the vamp this night, wearing a form-fitting black gown that exposed a portion of her very toned back.

A magical moment came when James Cromwell accepted the award in the best actor film category for his work in Still Mine.
