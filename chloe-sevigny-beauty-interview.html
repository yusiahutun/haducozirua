Chlo Sevigny on creating her own signature scent and tackling sustainability in beauty

 Posted on March 5, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  956 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The queen of indie cinema and embodiment of the cool girl construct, Chloë Sevigny is loved as much for her fashion as her filmography. From vintage Levi’s to eclectic dresses, her nonchalant style can claim iconic status, along with her laidback blonde and bold red lips - both distinctively Sevigny. And, as you can imagine, behind the lens, there's an inimitable signature scent.

“I’ve worn fragrance since high school basically,” Sevigny tells us, having found The One years ago in Rose by Commes de Garçons – until it was discontinued. “I literally had a panic attack, I had to find it on eBay and I collected as many bottles as I could. Everyone recognised me by that scent.” Wedded to the note, she tried Hermès' Rose Ikebana as a replacement but, while nice, it didn’t weigh up. Naturally, the actress jumped at the chance to create her very own scent with her good friends, craftsmen Alia Raza and Ezra Woods of Régime des Fleurs (think artisan fragrances inspired by nature, history and art). Consider the result, Little Flower, the chicest anti-cliché celebrity fragrance.

“This perfume is surprisingly very fresh, very dewy,” she says, likening its trail to “being in a rose garden in bloom”. There’s a crispness from pepper and blackcurrant, with a woody dry down thanks to palo santo. “So it starts very fresh and becomes a little warmer.”

Sevigny says scent is less of a confidence tool and more one of comfort for her. “I think when you wear one every day it becomes part of you, it’s protective,” she muses.

The actress douses herself in it. “I usually apply when my skin is still a little moist out of the shower. Apparently if you apply creams and perfumes when you’re still a little damp they penetrate better. I do three squirts around my neck, two on my wrists, then I get dressed and I do another four spritzes, because I like it also to stay on my clothes. I feel like fragrance stays better on clothing and in dry hair.”

Available now at Dover Street Market, before launching into Liberty London, it’s exactly what you’d expect from Sevigny: considered yet confident and originally stylish. It slots nicely into her personal beauty wardrobe, a high-low edit. “I tend to shop for beauty products at health food stores,” she reveals. “They carry reliable brands like Dr Hauschka and Weleda, but it depends on what I’m looking for.” Weleda’s Skin Food is a staple in her skincare regime, as is Smith’s Rosebud Salve, Dr Bronner’s Soap and the Tatcha products. “I use their face wash, the rice polishing powder, the moisturiser, and the dewy mist that sets your make-up and makes it look less powdery – it’s all very luxurious.”

While this would make for an impressive skincare ‘shelfie’, her make-up bag is minimal. “I don’t really like it,” she admits, “apart from lipstick and mascara which is easy”. Maybelline’s Great Lash mascara is responsible for her always on point, inky black lashes, and her trademark orangy-red lip has a story in its own right.

Getty Images

“For years I wore MAC’s Lady Danger, it’s a matte formula so you have to use a primer and it gets very dry.” Then she was approached by French company La Bouche Rouge which make sustainable lipstick tubes (sold here at Harrods). “I struggle when buying cosmetics and products that everything comes in plastic – it’s very hard to find things that aren’t, and they have this luxury item whereby you’re spending more but you’re also getting really high quality,” she explains. She was asked to make a signature red for their refillable tubes. “I tested five or six, maybe more, reds to find the perfect one [called Le Rouge Chloe], and the texture and scent is really good. It goes on matte, but your lips still stay moist.”

While she finds sustainable shopping of cosmetics “almost impossible”, Sevigny definitely does her bit. “I heard that Aesop is going to go back to all-glass again, which I think is great. And I like Susanne Kaufmann, she uses all glass for her product packaging.” The actress also tries to buy “from brands that are hopefully maintaining some sort of conscious capitalism,” she adds, "but whoever really knows”.

Making sustainable luxury attainable is a challenge she also recognises. When it comes to Little Flower, she tells us “we’d like to make some products that are more accessible, that aren’t as expensive, like a smaller version or maybe even a cream or candle so that more can experience the beautiful fragrance that we created”.

The 44-year-old tells us that lifestyle is a much a factor in her beauty philosophy as her product consumption. Despite having tried “everything on the menu basically” from her dermatologist Dr David Colbert (who offers the likes of laser facials and Botox), she’s not consistent with treatments.

“I’m more about from the inside-out; diet and exercise and drinking lots of water. I’m about stuff in moderation, but I try to keep it pretty healthy. Apart from the sun!” She does wear SPF daily and explains, “I try and wear a hat now all the time in the sun,” but only at the beach – not in the city. “I don’t want to look like one of those crazy people who is obsessed with their skin.” Though no doubt she'd look crazy-cool, the way only Chloë can.
