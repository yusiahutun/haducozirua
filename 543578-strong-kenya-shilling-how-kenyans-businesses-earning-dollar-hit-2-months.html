Strong Kenya Shilling: How Kenyans, Businesses Earning In US Dollar Have Been Hit In 2 Months

 Posted on July 30, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  698 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon
  • Kenyans and businesses earning in US dollars have seen the value of their income decline since February 2024
  • Tables turned when the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) propped up the shilling against the US dollar, which saw the shilling appreciate
  • Financial consultant Walter Tarus, the founder and managing partner of WealthWise Financial Consulting Ltd, urged Kenyans hoarding dollars to sell them

Since November 2023, Tom Rashid Juma, an online content service provider, has been pocketing KSh 10,000 to KSh 20,000 on top of his salary.

Why Kenyan shilling strengthened against US dollar

However, the tables started turning in February 2024, when the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) propped up the shilling against the United States (US) dollar.

The regulator raised the base lending rate by 50 basis points, from 12.5% to 13%, a level last seen in the final years of Mwai Kibaki's presidency.

This, coupled with forex inflows to pay off the $2 billion Eurobond, saw the shilling strengthen against the greenback to be ranked one of the best-performing currencies in the world.

On Wednesday, the shilling traded at 131.48, down from a high of 157.3 on January 3, according to CBK's official rate.

With the local currency's appreciation, Juma disclosed to TUKO.co.ke that his salary has decreased by 15% since he's paid in US dollars.

"I have been hit by over a 15% pay cut in the last two months since the shilling started gaining strength against the US dollar. The more the shilling strengthens, the more my monthly pay reduces. I tried reaching out to my employer about the issue, hoping to get a pay rise, but he insisted that he can only increase the amount once the shilling reaches equilibrium against the US dollar (about KSh 120 per unit)," Juma revealed.

Kenyans working online struggle

Other Kenyans working online and receiving salaries in dollars also intimated to TUKO.co.ke that they were struggling to pay bills amid a weak dollar.

William Ngarachu, a freelance online data analyst, said his purchasing power had reduced following the decline in value of his earnings.

"As the shilling gains strength, it's like a catastrophe. The amount paid in dollars stays constant, lowering the amount in shillings. The issue now arises when the value of the shilling increases while commodity prices stay unchanged. In essence, nothing has changed regarding the economy, which lowers our purchasing power and makes things more difficult to accomplish. It's comparable to receiving a wage reduction when you were heavily dependent on your prior income and had many plans with it," Ngarachu said in an exclusive interview.

Collins Kiptoo, an academic writer, was another Kenyan whose salary was affected.

Kiptoo said bills consume his salary, leaving little or nothing for savings.

"The appreciation of the shilling against the dollar is really affecting me. Previously, when the dollar was stronger against the shilling, I could comfortably pay my bills and still have a good amount left for savings. However, now my bills remain the same while the amount I receive after converting my earnings to Kenyan shillings is decreasing day by day. At times, I have no money left to save since the amount I used to receive has decreased, while the bills have remained constant," he said.

Why you should sell US dollars

Financial consultant Walter Tarus, the founder and managing partner of WealthWise Financial Consulting Ltd, urged Kenyans hoarding dollars to sell them.

He noted that importers who ordered goods at a high dollar rate were counting losses.

"Thos hoarding dollars should sell. Manufacturers as well have been affected if the raw materials were imported at a high rate. The employees who earn in dollars have seen a cut in their disposable income due to dollar depreciation hence, if they had taken loans, they may need to seek repayment plan rescheduling with their banks," Tarus told TUKO.co.ke.
