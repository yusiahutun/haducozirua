EPS building technology: benefits, house designs, and cost in Kenya

 Posted on August 1, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  780 words  |  Martina Birk

EPS building technology is one of the alternative construction technologies today. The EPS panels are versatile and can be used for various construction applications because of their lightweight nature, stability, and strength.

The EPS technology is among the cheapest in Kenya, and the panels are available in a wide range. This ensures flexibility in design and in selecting various options when constructing. The panels are ideal for ceiling, walling, and slab.

What is EPS building technology?

EPS stands for Expanded Polystyrene. It is an economical, modern, efficient, and safe construction system. The EPS panels are white foam plastic materials made from solid polystyrene beads. They are manufactured in a factory and then transported to the construction site for assembly.

How much does EPS building technology cost in Kenya?

The EPS technology in Kenya is quickly gaining popularity because of its affordability. It's cheaper than conventional stones yet so solid and durable.

What are the benefits of EPS building technology?

The EPS building technology has considerable benefits over other construction materials. Here are the five most notable benefits.

1. EPS has excellent dimensional stability

Most plastic polymers expand or shrink when exposed to temperature change or humidity. But EPS has excellent dimensional stability and remains unaffected by different environmental conditions. The panels deliver consistently for their entire lifetime.

2. EPS panels are lightweight

EPS is usually about 80% air, making it one of the lightest construction materials. The lightweight nature of EPS panels reduces fuel costs which makes transportation easy. It also means job site handling will be simple and quick.

3. EPS offers design flexibility

EPS technology offers you a variety of excellent design flexibility. They can accommodate curved or tall walls, long ceiling spans, large openings, cathedral ceilings, and custom angles. Foam is easy to shape and cut, so contractors can build custom angles or curved walls without worrying about structural load.

4. EPS panels are easy to use

EPS panels can help maximize construction efficiency. The lightweight material can easily be custom cut, stacked, or maneuvered onsite or during manufacturing to fill design specifications. EPS is safe to handle without additional safety gear or special construction equipment.

In addition, products like EPS foam blocks can be used in different weather conditions minimizing construction delays.

5. EPS installation doesn’t need specialized labor

Like traditional brick/ masonry construction, EPS panels can be installed using the usual caliber of labor. The construction workers that'll install the panels need light training. This training will include setting and aligning panels, installing slab panels and floor, etc.

What are the disadvantages of EPS building technology?

In as much as EPS technology has a lot of benefits, it also has some shortcomings. These are some known disadvantages of EPS wall panels.

1. EPS panels production isn’t environment friendly

The production process of EPS panels isn't environmentally friendly. It releases many harmful gas and water that significantly impact the environment.

2. EPS panels are non-recyclable

The EPS technology involves polystyrene, an oil-based product that's not easily recyclable on an industrial scale. As such, when EPS foam ends in nature, it's not degradable; instead, it atomizes into small parts that are harmful to animals and humans in the long run.
