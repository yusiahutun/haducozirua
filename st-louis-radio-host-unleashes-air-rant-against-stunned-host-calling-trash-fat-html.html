St. Louis radio host FIRED over off-air rant against his co-host - calling her a 'f**king fat b**ch'

 Posted on July 21, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  783 words  |  Martina Birk

A St. Louis radio host was fired after he went on a rampage about his female co-host, calling her a 'terrible mom' and that she was 'trash, fat, and stupid' in off-air rant after she criticized his computer skills. 

Vic Faust, a former Fox 2 News anchor, was fired after he went off in a rage-fueled, sexist rant about his co-host Crystal Cooper while hosting a radio show on September 13, Chief Communications Officer of Nexstar Media, Gary Weitman, told DailyMail.com on Wednesday. 

His profanity-filled rampage criticized Cooper's parenting skills and her weight, according the St. Louis Post-Dispatch, who obtained an audio recording of the off-air rant that happened during a commercial break. 

'Listen, you're a f**king fat, nasty, b**ch. And you don't f**king bring up s**t up on-air when I f**king had your back, you're a f**king, fat, b**ch,' he raged during the show, according to an edited clip released by the Dispatch. 

The original recording of the show, which is posted on YouTube, does not appear to have the off-air rage in it anymore. 

When Cooper told him this is the second time he has called her 'stupid,' he angrily told her: 'You were making fun of me while I was messing with the computer.'

'You have a history of attacking me on-air,' he continued. 'You're a fat, self-righteous, b**ch. And every day you come into this f**king studio, I will f**king call you what you are: a f**king fat nasty b**ch.'
