What are government securities?

Government securities are financial instruments issued by a government to raise funds and are considered very low-risk investments because the full faith and credit of the issuing government backs them.

According to the CBK, the government issues two types of government securities:

1. Treasury bills

Treasury bills offer short-term investment opportunities, with maturities ranging from 91 days to 364 days.

Ifyou invest in a Treasury bill, you can expect to recoup your investment within three months, six months, or one year, depending on the bill's duration.

Investors profit from Treasury bills through discounted purchase prices. For instance, if you opt for a 91-day Treasury bill, you will pay less than its face value initially. However, you'll receive the full face value upon the bill's maturity in 91 days.

A minimum investment of KSh 100,000 is required to acquire a Treasury bill.

2. Treasury bonds

Treasury bonds are medium—to long-term investment options, with maturity durations spanning from one year to 30 years. While various types of Treasury bonds exist, their fundamental workings remain similar.

Investors engaging in Treasury bond transactions essentially lend funds to the government for a predetermined duration, corresponding to the bond's maturity.

Typically, investors receive biannual interest payments throughout the bond's tenure, culminating in the repayment of the invested face value upon maturity.

To acquire a Treasury bond, a minimum investment of Kshs. 50,000 is requisite.

How to invest in government securities in Kenya

To invest in government securities, Kenyans must register online for the Central Securities Depository (DhowCSD) user account.

To register, investors can access the Central Bank of Kenya website at www.centralbank.go.ke or download the DhowCSD app from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
