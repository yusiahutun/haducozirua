Football's 10 most profitable academies with Man City only third best in Premier League

 Posted on April 27, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  607 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Chelsea lead the way as the most profitable youth academy in the Premier League – with Manchester City down in third.

A list of the clubs which have recouped the most from selling homegrown talent between 2014 and 2023 has been published by the Football Observatory research group. Three English clubs make the top 10 – Chelsea, Tottenham and Man City.

The Blues’ status as England’s top seller of talent should come as no surprise given their long-standing model of developing players, loaning them out to gain experience and then flogging them for profit. In recent years, they have sold the likes Tammy Abraham, Fikayo Tomori and Mason Mount for big money.

Chelsea are fifth overall, with Spurs eighth and City ninth. Tottenham will have shot up the list thanks to the £86million sale of Harry Kane to Bayern Munich last year. City boast an impressive youth set-up which produced the likes Phil Foden and Cole Palmer, with the latter being sold to Chelsea for £45million last year.

The club with the most profitable youth set-up – and by some distance – are Benfica, who have banked some huge fees over the last 10 years. The Portuguese giants received just over £100million from Atletico Marid for Joao Felix in 2019, around £69million from Paris Saint-Germain for Goncalo Ramos last year and £62million from Man City for Ruben Diaz in 2020.

The Lisbon club also received hefty fees for the likes of Renato Sanches (from Bayern Munich), Goncalo Guedes (from PSG) and Bernard Silva (from Monaco) during the period covered in the research.

Next up is that well-known factory of youth talent, Ajax, who have flogged the likes of Matthijs de Ligt (to Juventus), Jurrien Timber (to Arsenal) and Manchester United flop Donny van de Beek. French side Lyon are third on the list with Alexandra Lacazette to Arsenal, for just over £50million, their biggest sale.

Real Madrid are next in fourth and are the only Spanish side to make the top 10, which also features Monaco, Sporting Lisbon and Atalanta.

The latter, the only Italian club in the top 10, have made fortunes selling players, including Rasmus Hojlund to Manchester United, to the Premier League and the Serie A big guns like Juventus and the Milan sides.
