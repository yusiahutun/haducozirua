    Hello! Fashion shares the fashion collaborations that everyone is talking about right now: 

    Charles & Keith x Chet Lo

    Famed footwear brand Charles & Keith has teamed up with up-and-coming funky fashion giant Chet Lo to create a collection of shoes, accessories and ready-to-wear. The shoe section of the collection includes platforms, ballet flats and heels, all boasting Chet Lo's famous spike texture. Each shoe is available in three colourways, including hot pink and deep red. Both of the two bag options are also available in the same colourways where the clothing capsule introduces grey a longsleeve shirt, a vibrant red maxi skirt and a chic black mini skirt.

    Shop now

    HUSHU/TONG × Asics

    It seems Asics aren't stopping anytime soon on the collaboration front and I couldn't be more thrilled, especially when their latest silhouette is in dainty baby blue gingham. Teasing on Instagram late last month, Asics announced their partnership with SHUSHU/TONG, sharing an image of the iconic Tarther SC Plus style, reinvented with frills and buckles. The new silhouette was made available on December 24th, sold exclusively at the ASICS flagship store in Shanghai, however there's no word on when, or if, they're going to be made available for the rest of the world.

    Sézane x Pangea 

    Cult Parisian label Sézane has teamed up with artistic duo Colombine Jubert & Laëtitia Rouget, known as Pangea, on a travel-focused collection which celebrates both colour and creativity. Shot on a blustery beach, the campaign is gorgeous, very in keeping with Sézane's easy-breezy elegance but with a splash of vibrancy. The prints are playful and unpretentious, but I've fallen hard for the warm, brassy statement jewellery.

    Sézane x Pangea

    Asics x Kiko Kostadinov

    The rumours are true! Kiko Kostadinov and Asics are teaming up to bring us 2024's most cultivated ballet flat and it's set to be a sell-out. At this point we don't have much info on the release, all we know is that the sporty ballet pump is in a black colourway with grey accent star stitching and comes with lots of metallic elastic straps. Earlier this week Kiko Kostadinov posted a picture of the she to their Instagram, simply captioning it "2024".

    Koibird X Maison Games

    Just in time for the inevitable friends and family Christmas game nights, Koibird has teamed up with the world's chicest backgammon set makers, Maison Games, to produce a neon pink set worthy of displaying on the coffee table. The collab is available to shop now on the Koibird website but get in there quick because they won't be around for long. 

    Koibird x Maison Games

    Ganni x Dr. Martens

    When it comes to collaborations, cult favourite Danish fashion brand Ganni has been turning them around quicker than quick. Right off the back of their Barbour and 66 North collabs, Ganni has teamed up with Dr. Martens to make the ultimate winter boot, reimagining the iconic Jadon Boot crafted from a blend of recycled polyester and leather-free hard-wearing alternative materials

    Ugg x Palace Skateboards

    Just in time for winter, Ugg has teamed up with Palace Skateboards to reinvent their famed Tasman silhouette. Aside from slippers, the collection also features shearling mittens and a ‘P’ shaped sheepskin rug, perfect for those hype beast home lovers.

    Rick Owens x Moncler

    Pioneers in luxury winterwear, Moncler have teamed up with one of fashion's most iconic weird girl brands, Rick Owens to create a collection of futuristic puffers, quilted jackets, boots, scarves and blankets.

    Ford x Dickies x Sydney Sweeney

    To the untrained eye, this collaboration might look a little peculiar, but real ones will know that this merging of icons is as on-brand as it gets. For the second time this year Euphoria star and mechanic Sydney Sweeney teams up with Dickies and Ford to create a line of wildly chic workwear. The collection features a range of denim overalls, baby tees, caps and worker jackets. The collaboration hopes to encourage women car enthusiasts after research showed that only 2% of mechanics are women.

    Anya Hindmarch x Uniqlo

    Uniqlo and the British label Anya Hindmarch have revealed their winter capsule collection for 2023. This collection showcases reimagined Uniqlo knitwear with intricate craftsmanship and artistic touches. It includes luxurious 100 per cent cashmere pieces that are not only warm but also lightweight and incredibly soft. The collection also features Anya Hindmarch's's signature eye motif embroidery, adding a unique and playful element to the designs. 

    “I’m a huge Uniqlo fan and admire the beauty in its practicality which resonates with how I like to design," Anya Hindmarch explained in a statement, "I decided not to mess with their brilliant silhouettes, but instead to play with them; deconstructing and then rebuilding them with different stitches and exposed seams – and in turn leaving all sort of little design details to make pulling on your knitwear the favorite part of your day."

    Mango x Jen Ceballo

    Mango just added another name to their already long list of standout collaborations, this time calling upon the uber-chic Miami-based influencer, Jen Ceballo. Just in time for the party season ahead, the collection features a range of metallic tones, lace dresses, satin fabrics, heeled peep-toe sandals, and a range of stand-out accessories. If you’re on the hunt for the perfect party ensemble that boasts bold glamour then this is it.

    H&M x Rabanne 

    Party season couldn't come any sooner. Infused with glitz and celebratory spirit, the newly rebranded 'Rabanne' has partnered with high street retailer H&M for the collab to end all collabs. Expect flashes of chainmail and disc sequins in abundance. Don't miss out – we reckon the resale prices will be astronomical.

    Jorja Smith x Clarks Originals 

    Singer-songwriter Jorja Smith has teamed up with Clarks to reimagine the British footwear stalwart's signature Desert Nomad shoes in mule form. The result is a laid-back profile, crafted from Sciarada Evolo suede – boasting a more sustainable footprint compared to traditional suede – and steeped in casual cool. 

    Fenty x Puma 

    Rihanna has returned to the Puma fold, and we couldn't be gladder. The multi-hyphenate and Fenty founder has rejoined forces with the sportswear giant, and the 'Avanti' shoe, blending soft, supple leather with gum outsoles, pays tribute to Puma's rich heritage. A future footwear icon, if ever we saw one.

    Jimmy Choo x Jean Paul Gaultier 

    Fusing the identities of two iconic fashion labels, this footwear collection created by Jimmy Choo and Jean Paul Gaultier will go down in collaboration history. A celebration of their collective ideology of "glamorous rebellion", the campaign features Kylie Minogue and the shoes – ranging from Swarovski pumps and thigh-high boots – are both off-beat and interesting. 

    Naomi Campbell x Pretty Little Thing

    Paying tribute to the legendary supermodel's unique sense of style, fast fashion retailer Pretty Little Thing has collaborated with Naomi Campbell on a collection that is truly perfect for party season. Designed by Naomi alongside emerging talents Victor Anate and Edvin Thompson, the capsule features sleek blazers, cool-girl cut-outs, sultry mesh detailing and an abundance of sequins. 

    Sienna Miller x M&S 

    Actress Sienna Miller is starring in British high street hero M&S' new AW23 campaign 'Anything But Ordinary', and we are totally here for it. Featuring a broad range of key pieces, from tweed blazers and tailored waistcoats to acid green knits and fuzzy checked coats, the pieces feel fresh and wearable. Frankly, we'd expect nothing less from the fashion icon...

    Tada & Toy x LK Bennett 

    British high street stalwart LK Bennett has joined forces with London-based demi-fine jewellery brand Tada & Toy for an exclusive eight-piece collection. Fusing Tada & Toy's playful celestial aesthetic with LK Bennett's rich metallic tones, pieces include: glitzy star-shaped hoops, delicate ear cuffs, and cute shooting star climbers.

    Sandro x Wrangler

     Luxury Parisian brand Sandro has partnered with denim specialist Wrangler on a 10-piece collection inspired by 1970s cowboy looks. Celebrating Wrangler's heritage via three key details - flat rivets, flap seams and W-shaped pocket topstitching - the pieces were conceived by Sandro's menswear studio, but with the intention of the collection not being confined to traditional gender boundaries. 

    Adanola x Steph Shep 

    Manchester-based activewear label Adanola has teamed up with influencer, and former assistant to Kim Kardashian, Steph Shep for a capsule collection. Designed specifically to "fill the gaps that Steph felt she had in her active, everyday wardrobe", the collection features sleek quarter zips, wearable off-the-shoulder tops and cool-girl vests. 

    Free People x Angie Smith

    Contemporary British style guru and Hello! Fashion contributor Angie Smith has curated her "perfect autumn edit" for cool-girl boho label Free People. Consisting of warm, earthy-hued everyday staples, the collection offers a wealth of easy-breezy outfit inspiration. Expect Western-style boots, versatile cream separates and fringing aplenty. 

    Terry de Havilland x Annie's Ibiza

    Devotees of both brands are in for a treat with this limited-edition collaboration. Terry de Havilland have joined forces with Annie’s Ibiza, the sought-after destination store for carefully chosen vintage and designer items. With a shared ethos of crafting vintage-inspired gems the partnership has led to the creation six exclusive cowboy boots. These  distinct styles blend the rich history and carefree spirit of the Seventies and feature the iconic shoe brand's signature opulent textures. The boots are available in a range of colours, including: Black, White, Silver, Electric Blue, Crystal Red, and Crystal Black. The design incorporates the timeless leather cuts that characterise Terry de Havilland's famous high-heeled cowboy boots, as seen in the V&A Museum and notably, the lining boasts Annie’s Ibiza's signature orange, lending a bold splash of colour to the design.
