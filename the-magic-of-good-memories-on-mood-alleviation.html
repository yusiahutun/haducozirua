The Magic of Good Memories on Mood Alleviation

 Posted on August 8, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1351 words  |  Martina Birk

The poet John Keats popularised the expression “Moments big as years,” which relates to moments in time that are so strong and vivid that they stick with us for the rest of our lives. Typically, these instances represent emotionally impactful experiences. They give our lives purpose and joy, and they continue to benefit us long after they have happened, adding to our overall well-being and resiliency. They provide unending joy; they are a gift. Positive memories are actively recalled, and people who do so enjoy life more.

Comparatively to those who did not intentionally engage in the practice, individuals who actively engaged in reflecting on and reliving good memories reported higher levels of contentment and satisfaction. Nostalgia, or sentimental fondness for the past that has pleasant personal associations, was proven to be a powerful happiness enhancer. It turns out that nostalgia has the capacity to increase one’s sense of self, magnify positive feelings, and even serve as a defense mechanism against unfavourable thoughts.

Psychological Benefits of Good Memories

Life well-being has been found to be significantly influenced by happy memories. Simply said, the capacity to warmly recall former experiences, circumstances, and people can bring joy and comfort when you least expect it.

1) Positive emotions

Photographs are a true treasure trove that instantly takes us back to particular joyful occasions in our past. When it comes to pleasantly stimulating our memory, photographs—whether they be of our families, ourselves, or other people—work faster than magic. They don’t just preserve a memory from the past; they also provide us with a hundred happy moments each time we see that picture. The majority of pictures either make people laugh, cry, or simply want to share them with someone special. No matter what context the snapshot contains, the response is almost always favourable.

2) Stress Reduction

Good memories are a saviour is our stressful times in life. When we are under stress and think back on happy memories from our past, such as successes, family vacations, or laughter, it provides us more energy to go forwards in a positive manner. It induces a relaxation reaction, lifts our spirits, and reduces worry.

3) Increases sense of gratitude

When you think back on happy and wonderful memories, you frequently feel grateful. Such positive mood swings can last for the better part of a day. Such a happy recollection is particularly potent since it inspires thankfulness for life and one’s surroundings.

4) Increases self-esteem

Remembering or recalling times of extreme happiness and delight is frequently associated with periods of success for many people. For instance, perhaps you were the year’s top student or the winner of the school race. Or perhaps you stayed up late working on a school assignment that the principal praised. These kinds of joyful recollections inspire you to do your best effort in the present as well as make you happy. Additionally, they encourage us to build on our past successes and possibly surpass them.

5) Relationship satisfaction

The emotional and social ties between people can be strengthened through happy memories associated with loved ones. Reliving all the wonderful interactions can strengthen the sense of community among people.

Health Benefits of Recalling Good Memories

Reliving happy memories has many more advantages for your health than just momentary delight. According to studies, reliving joyful memories on purpose can assist to break up negative thought patterns, reduce anxiety, and even lower cortisol levels. Because it encourages a more positive self-image, this practice has been particularly useful with young people who are at risk for depression owing to stress:-

1) Improved sleep:

Stressful events and passing bad feelings are a part of everyday life, which interfere with good sleep patterns and cause insomnia or poor sleep quality. Before going to bed, if we concentrate on happy memories, we can divert our thoughts from unhappy ones to happy ones. This improves the quantity and quality of our sleep.

2) Immune system functioning:

Positive memories have been demonstrated to improve our immune system performance, increase the creation of antibodies, and reduce inflammation, making us healthier people.

3) Cardiovascular functioning:

A person’s cardiovascular system can be hampered by negative emotions, which can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease, and stroke. Recalling happy memories regulates blood flow and enhances heart health, which has a positive effect on the cardiovascular system.

A Few Research evidence
  • While undergoing functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) scanning, Speer et al. (2014) gave participants a cue recall task to complete. The exercise required the participants to recollect their own autobiographical memories in response to positive (such as a family vacation or a trip to Disneyland) and neutral (such as packing for a trip) life event cues. In line with earlier studies, Speer et al. (2014) discovered that remembering happy memories led to happier sentiments and more emotional intensity than recovering unhappy memories.
  • Research done in 2019, suggests that recalling specific positive events may promote mental health, and resilience, especially in the face of stress in teenagers at risk of depression.
  • The association between the memory of happy memories and mood was investigated in a 2012 study that appeared in the Journal of Happiness Studies. The study’s findings showed that people who actively reflected on happy past experiences had higher positive mood states and lower negative mood states. This shows that revisiting pleasant memories can elevate mood in general.
    • Techniques to engage in recalling positive memories
    1) Visual imagery
    • Make a list of some good memories, then pick one to think about.
    • Close your eyes, relax, take a nice, deep breath, and start thinking about the recollection.
    • Allow yourself to recall any imagery associated with the recollection. Consider the people you saw and the things they were saying, doing, and wearing.
    • Remember where you were and what made the memory so great while paying attention to the sounds around you and making them as vivid as you can. Give yourself permission to enjoy this luxury of time.
    2) Capitalising

    According to research, when we share our pleasant experiences with others, they become more meaningful and, as a result, improve our well-being. This is referred to as sharing or capitalization.

    Therefore, locate a companion, share the idea of happy memories with them, and then alternate sharing happy memories. To enhance the joyful sensation, you can even ponder the pleasant memory.

    3) Making it visual

    Are there any things or locations in your own life that have taken on special meaning for you? A particular object that someone gave us or manufactured for us, a card or letter, something we built or developed, or just something that reminds us of a wonderful time—a ticket stub, seashell, rock, or souvenir—can all serve as examples.

    Create a work of visual art that depicts a happy moment in time or a period of time. Include tangible elements such as photography, poetry, pictures, and items. Another method of putting happy memories into visual form is through scrapbooking and photo cluster displays.

    4) Use statements like ‘ Remember When ’ frequently

    Talk about “remember when” with your pals. Practice “remembering when” the next time you’re among family and friends by thinking back on enjoyable occasions that have forged stronger bonds.

    5) Apply Prompts

    Utilise the power of sensory triggers, such as sound or smell, to take yourself back to enjoyable memories from the past. Your favorite song from your formative years has the capacity to instantly transport you back to a happy period of your life. The aroma of baked bread might bring back memories of the family and home. You can quickly return to a good memory by using these sensory cues. A bottle of my late mother’s perfume can frequently transport me back in time to a special, long-gone period of my life.
