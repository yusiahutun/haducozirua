Uproar as Senators Sniff Fertilizer to Determine Whether its Real or Fake

 Posted on June 23, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  509 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill
  • Senators have sparked a social media uproar after appearing to be sniffing fertiliser to test its authenticity
  • Fake fertiliser samples were presented before the Senate Agricultural Committee for investigation
  • Agriculture CS Mithika Linturi said there was no fake fertiliser in the market, adding that only substandard fertilisers have been spotted

A video of senators sniffing a fertiliser sample to determine whether it is real or fake has sparked reactions on social media.

During a parliamentary proceeding where Agriculture Cabinet Secretary Mithika Linturi appeared before the Senate Agricultural Committee, samples of fake fertilizer were brought before senators.

The lawmakers were seen sniffing at the samples one after the other, raising concerns over their safety, considering the authenticity of the fertiliser was already in question.

How to test whether fertiliser is legit or fake

On Thursday, April 4, Farmers Association of Kenya Chair Bimal Kantaria stated that it was close to impossible to determine whether fertilizer is real or fake by just looking at it.

Kantaria added that for one to get accurate results, the suspected fertilizer sample has to be tested.

Although most Kenyans have no access to facilities that can help assess the suitability of a fertliser, they can check out both the physical and chemical properties of the fertilizer.

For instance, when buying fertiliser one can look at the packaging, seal, components of the fertiliser and information about the distributor.

If any of these physical traits of the fertilizer appear to be tampered with, then one should proceed to look for the fertilizer that meets the physical standards.

One should also check the chemical composition of the fertilizer, for instance, NPK 9:16:6, or 17:17:17.

CS Mithika Linturi Says Fertiliser is not Fake

Agriculture Cabinet Secretary Mithika Linturi has maintained that there is no fake fertiliser in the Kenyan market.

Appearing before the Senate Agriculture Committee on Thursday, April 11, Linturi claimed the fertiliser was only substandard.

He pointed out that failing to meet the composition ratio does not render the fertiliser fake.
