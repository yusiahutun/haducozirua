The 10 Best Back Acne Treatments

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  12 minutes  |  2393 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

There are two types of back acne in the world — one is hormonal and the other is sweat-related. I’m a member of the latter group. And I often find myself struggling with back acne in the winter months. You’d think that it would flare up more in the summer months, but I notice that it gets worse in the winter when my back is trapped under heavy sweaters and jackets with no room to breathe. I find myself reaching for my back acne-treatment arsenal all winter (think: body washes, sprays, pads, and much more). 

Whether you deal with hormonal back acne, bacteria-fueled back acne, or somewhere in between, I’ve tested the best back acne treatments on the market and listed my faves below. A sure winner is the First Aid Beauty FAB Pharma White Clay Acne Treatment Pads 2% Salicylic Acid, which works to effectively fight back acne with one easy swipe each day.

What to Keep in Mind

Active Ingredients

One of the most important things to consider when choosing a back acne treatment is the active ingredients. “Look for something with active ingredients that will treat the acne you have right now,” explains Dr. Corey L. Hartman, a board-certified dermatologist and founder of Skin Wellness Dermatology in Birmingham, AL. “Look for a treatment that can help prevent acne from forming, either by killing acne bacteria, or expediting cell turnover.”

Your Skin Type

You also want to consider your skin type — do you have dry skin, oily skin, or combination skin? “Choosing a product, or regimen of products, that complement your natural skin type will be most helpful in regulating back acne,” explains Hartman.

Over-the-counter vs. Prescription Treatment

Finally, you want to see a professional if you think your back acne might be severe. Dr. Anar Mikailov, a board-certified dermatologist, founder of KP Away, and co-founder of Skintensive, suggests starting with a visit to your dermatologist to determine the cause of your acne. “If you are using a hammer where a screwdriver is needed, then it does not matter what brand or ingredients you use,” he says. “Make sure you understand the cause of the problem first, then look for the right solution.” 

Either your dermatologist will recommend over-the-counter products that contain ingredients like sulfur, salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, or retinoids, or they will prescribe you something else. “Some prescription products will be a combination of these ingredients and work very well,” says Mikailov.

Your Questions, Answered

What causes back acne?

Like face breakouts, back acne can be due to several different causes, according to Mikailov. “In the teenage years, back acne is usually hormonal, identical to the acne seen on the face,” he says. “Other causes of back acne include pityrosporum (yeast), medication-induced acne (such as steroids, lithium, or chemotherapy), hormonal acne post-adolescence (in women age 20 or greater and around menstruation), acne fulminans, acne conglobata, folliculitis due to pseudomonas or other bacteria or other acne mimickers.”

You might notice that your back acne flares up when you’re sweating more often. “Back acne is worsened when you wear sweaty clothing for an extended period of time, or have a lot of friction on the back caused by anything from wearing a backpack to workout equipment,” explains Hartman. “Back acne is most common on the upper back and shoulders as you have more sebaceous glands in those areas — sebaceous glands are responsible for the production of oil.”

What's the best treatment for back acne?

Treatment depends on how severe your back acne is. “If you have mild back acne, the best treatment is a benzoyl peroxide body wash — benzoyl peroxide kills the p.acnes bacteria that causes acne and it helps to clear out pores,” says Hartman. “Use it daily and wash your back with a clean washcloth (make sure it’s white in color, as benzoyl peroxide can bleach dark fabrics).” If you have severe back acne, which is often tied to hormones, Hartman’s patients see success with the oral medication called Spironolactone.

Another easy treatment for back acne is to try extra hard to avoid it from forming in the first place. “If you work out, shower and clean your back immediately—don’t sit in sweaty clothing for any longer than you need to,” suggests Hartman. “Make sure to take a shower to remove sunscreen after being outside, as sunscreen can clog pores and lead to back acne.”
