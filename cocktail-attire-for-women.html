Evening Outerwear

Edward Berthelot//Getty Images

"Do switch it up depending on the season," Choi says. "For the winter months, I like the idea of wearing an elegant evening coat as the highlight of the look and pairing that with a simple slip dress or a pair of sleek dress pants." Satin, velvet and brocades are all fair game when playing with this styling piece, and these lighter weight fabrics can be worn throughout spring and fall, as well.

Silky Suiting

Jeremy Moeller//Getty Images

"A pantsuit is always a solid option," Choi says. Whether it be a trouser or skirt suit, silky tailoring is a sure-fire way to do cocktail dressing right. Lean into the slight sheen of satin, charmeuse, and other silk textures that feel slinkier than your everyday wool, cotton or twill. The styling opportunities are endless (wear as a set, separately, with or without a blouse, the list goes on...), proving this is one classic investment you will reach for time and again.

Two-Piece Sets

Daniel Zuchnik//Getty Images

"For the summer months, I like two-piece sets that you can wear again as separates," Choi says. "One/Of nails this look every time!" However, the ease of a matching set transcends to all seasons. For instance, a sleeveless top and pant combo can be worn as is, layered with a turtleneck underneath, or topped with a blazer for colder nights. Just remember, don't be afraid to mix and match!
