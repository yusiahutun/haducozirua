Kathryn Kelly Parents

Kathryn Kelly was born to her father Corry Dickerson and her mother Susan Coleman Dickerson.

Her parents started dating each other when they were in UT Knoxville school in Atlanta. Kathryn’s mother shared a short story about her parents on her Facebook which was dedicated to Kathryn’s paternal grandfather Joe Dickerson Sr. She wrote, “Dating my husband (in Atlanta) after our UT Knoxville school days meant I didn’t know his family well at all. On visits to Knoxville– which were overnighters, of course, I found to break the nerves of awkwardness… I would just dive into whatever they were doing around the house and help out. It was my way to connect and have a conversation about things they were interested in such as his workshop or her cooking or his birdfeeder refills…etc.”

She continued, “One day Joe P. slung his arm around my shoulders and said… “Do you know what I like about you? You are a Do-er! I like that. That’s a good thing.”I thought hmmm … well good! hahaha, I’ve thought about that many times since. I can now recognize the doers in the world and find myself wanting to line up with those people!!”

Kathryn lost her paternal grandfather a while back but hasn’t revealed anything about him.

As for her mom, Susan Dickerson currently works as a health, wellness, nutritional cleansing coach and entrepreneur at Isagenix. She started her job there in July 2012. Moreover, she also worked as a volunteer at Atlanta Dream Center, independent consultant at Arbonne International, Area Leader of RiverStone Church, and Elementary Educator at Cobb County Schools. Susan was born to Ronald Coleman and Helen Coleman.

Kathryn Kelly Net Worth

As an actress and model, Kathryn Kelly gathered a net worth of nearly $250 thousand.

Actress Kathryn inherited her passion for movies from her mom Susan Dickerson. In 2012, Susan explained on her Facebook that “Going to the movies” has always been her passion. Also, she loved seeking “sets” to get inside and look behind the scenes has always been a fascination of her.

From the first time Susan set foot on location at Universal Studios, CA in 1983 with her family, she was taken by the fact that she was seeing the real “Gilligan’s Island”, the street from “Leave it to Beaver” and the house it was filmed. She never saw a TV show or film the same way.

Then, she explained that her daughter Kathryn has started her career working in the movies in 2012. She explained that Kathryn made it fun on a whole new level and even more exciting with her study and pursuit of film/on-camera acting.

Susan wrote, “I’m beginning to enjoy “movie going” at a whole new level after experiencing what goes on through her studies, auditions and “jobs” on set as well as having a built-in “movie-going lover” in the house. Getting to know film directors, casting directors, agents, photographers, fellow actors and parents who have been in support of this “dream”…has brought a whole new level of appreciation in realizing what it takes to bringing a creative thought to paper to the camera to the screen.”

Kathryn grew up in Atlanta, Georgia where she currently lives. She studied acting and theatre since 14 years old and has successfully worked in the commercial, film, and print division with Houghton Talent Agency since 2011. Her Facebook revealed that she studied Film Intensive at Kristen Shaw Acting Studio, Improvisation, on-camera audition at Drama Inc., and Communications at Kennesaw State University.

Some of her notable TV shows and movies Kathryn appeared on are Nashville (2018), The Originals (2017), Fist Fight (2017), Powers (2016), The Hunger Games: Catching Fire (2013), Today (2013), Local Talent (2012), and Camp Chippewa (2012).

Besides movies and TV shows, she also indulged in some modeling gigs.

Fun Fact: Kathryn is a huge fan of actress Audrey Hepburn.
