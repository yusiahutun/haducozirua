Miranda Lambert biography

The singer was born on 10th November 1983 in Longview, Texas, in the United States. She is the daughter of Richard Lee Lambert and Beverly June Hughes. She was named after her great grandmother, Lucy Miranda.

Who are Miranda Lambert siblings? She has one sibling, a younger brother whose name hasn't been revealed to the public. The singer was brought up in Lindale, Texas, together with his younger brother.

Initially, her father served as an on-campus undercover narcotics officer. He is also a former Dallas police officer. In the 1970s, he played country-rock in-band known as the Contraband. Rick and Bev first met while attending a camp at Southern Methodist University.

The duo worked together as private detectives on several high-profile cases. Unfortunately, their lives took a turn after the oil crises dampened the economy in Texas. As a result, the singer's family lost everything they owned.

Endorsements

The singer has received several endorsements with his fame. In 2009, she was chosen together with other two singers to become Cotton Inc's new faces during the campaign known as The Touch, The Feel of Cotton.

During this endorsement, the singer appeared in advertisements to promote cotton. The cotton website also features a free download of the full version of one of her hit songs, Fabric of My Life. In 2014, she received her second endorsement.

Her line of boots and shoes were released at the Designer Show Warehouse. In November 2014, she partnered with Chrysler's Ram trucks. She was given a customized 1500 Laramie Longhorn under the partnership. She auctioned it off and gave the proceedings to Lambert's Muttnation Foundation.

Philanthropy and MuttNation Foundation

The singer has taken part in different philanthropic causes. In 2009, she founded the MuttNation Foundation together with her mother. The foundation aims at offering rescue and shelter to stray animals.
