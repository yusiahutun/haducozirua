Ex-Warrington man Matt King: A death is inevitable unless rugby tackles drug culture

 Posted on September 18, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  278 words  |  Martina Birk

King, who is assistant coach at South Sydney Rabbitohs, insists it’s only a matter of time because the problem is so bad.

Super League is known to have its own headaches with the issue, while the NRL is currently facing a full-blown crisis.

Six New Zealand Warriors players have been suspended after five of them admitted to taking a cocktail of sleeping pills and energy drinks on a night out.

New Zealand international Manu Vatuvei has since been put on indefinite leave from the club on medical grounds.

Last year two Rabbitohs players, Dylan Walker and Aaron Gray, were put into induced comas after overdosing on a variety of painkillers.

King saw at first hand how close the duo came to death and fears it won’t be long before someone does lose their life.

The former Aussie Test winger said: “It will happen one day. The boys get to a state where they don’t know how many they’re taking or whatever it might be and then you start to mix prescription drugs.

“We were so close at South Sydney last year to facing that exact dilemma. Prescription drugs are a massive concern - it’s really scary.”

Former Bradford coach Matthew Elliott said he suspected Warriors players of abusing prescription drugs when he was coach of the New Zealand club two years ago.

But he said he didn’t have enough evidence to alert the NRL and admits it’s an issue that’s difficult for coaches to get on top of.

He said: “The last person that knows is the coach, because prescription drugs aren’t performance enhancers, they’re the exact opposite, they’re performance detractors.”
