See-through: Nigella Lawson, pictured leaving a London restaurant last night, has unwittingly allowed neighbours a glimpse into her bathroom

Domestic Goddess Nigella Lawson  has been bringing a heavenly vision into the lives of neighbours near her new £12million Chelsea home.

Nigella and her husband, Charles Saatchi, have spent a fortune turning a huge former warehouse near the King’s Road into a seven-bedroom mansion: the kitchen alone cost £500,000.

But during their extensive works in transforming the building, while retaining the 1870 facade, they seem to have overlooked their modesty.
Their new home includes a stylish loggia — a gallery open to the elements on one of the upper floors — with a luxurious bathroom beyond.

Shunning hoi polloi fittings such as frosted glass or, indeed, even a door, there is, I am told, a clear view into the room, allowing neighbours in the exclusive enclave to unwittingly glimpse Nigella au naturel.

‘It isn’t a case of deliberately looking in, but there is a view into the bathroom and anyone glancing in the direction of the loggia from nearby can glimpse what’s going on,’ says an observer.

It is, I am told, easy to see why Nigella has been called a ‘goddess’, but there is a downside: alarmingly, Saatchi can also be spotted on occasion.

‘With all the alterations they’ve made, this was one factor they clearly didn’t take into account,’ says a friend of a neighbour.

The amount of work on the house, involving countless delivery lorries arriving and departing was, as I revealed, a source of considerable annoyance to locals before the couple moved in. 

They famously left their Belgravia pad in Eaton Square after neighbours erected scaffolding blocking their view. Police were called after Saatchi, whose worth with brother Maurice is estimated at £120million, ordered his own workmen to remove it.

Former home: Nigella and Charles Saatchi moved from their Eaton Square home after scaffolding blocked their view

Nigella, whose cookery programmes, books and kitchenware range have made her wealthy in her own right, hooted with laughter when I informed her of her room with a view. ‘I had no idea we were overlooked. Obviously, I will investigate.’

Let’s hope the matter is resolved before Nigella has to resort to the black burkini that hid her voluptuous figure on Bondi Beach earlier this year.
