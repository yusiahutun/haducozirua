What Is Bradley Cooper's Net Worth? A Star Is Born Earnings, Salary, Gross

 Posted on June 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  438 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill
  • The incredible success of A Star Is Born has had a huge effect on Bradley Cooper's already impressive net worth.
  • The actor regularly commands huge salaries for his movie roles, and made $46 million in 2014 alone, per Forbes.

Bradley Cooper's star has been steadily growing since his unforgettable performance in Jennifer Garner's spy TV show Alias. And since transitioning to director and screenwriter with A Star Is Born, the Oscar-nominated actor has only seen his net worth continue to grow.

Here's everything you need to know about Bradley Cooper's net worth:

A Star Is Born was great for Cooper's finances.

Unsurprisingly, Cooper's directorial debut has already netted him a lot of money. According to Box Office Mojo, as of March 2019, A Star Is Born has so far grossed $433,869,279 worldwide. As the movie's budget was a modest $36 million, that's a huge profit margin. As the male lead, director, co-screenwriter, and co-songwriter on some of the movie's songs, Cooper is likely taking home a large amount of money for what can only be described as an Oscar-winning musical extravaganza.

He was doing pretty well before A Star Is Born came out.

The Hollywood Reporter revealed in 2015, "Following the success of American Sniper and his three Oscar nominations as an actor, Bradley Cooper is expected to command $15 million to $20 million a picture—lifting him above the $15 million he got for the third Hangover." Basically, Cooper had already become accustomed to securing a hefty payday for every movie he was in.

He made $46 million in 2014, and $41.5 million in 2015.

In 2014, Cooper made a whopping $46 million, per Forbes, thanks to roles in Silver Linings Playbook, American Hustle, and Guardians of the Galaxy.

And in 2015, Forbes revealed that Cooper made a total of $41.5 million due to appearances in American Sniper and Aloha, and Netflix's Wet Hot American Summer. The publication notes that Cooper also boasted earnings from "his production company, which he runs with Hangover director Todd Phillips."
