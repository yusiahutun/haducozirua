The 11 Best Ugg Slippers of 2024

 Posted on August 27, 2024  |  12 minutes  |  2388 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Known for its notoriously comfy boots, Ugg also delivers stylish, functional, and snuggly slippers. A long-time favorite brand of celebrities like Selena Gomez and Irina Shayk, the brand’s neutral footwear can be surprisingly easy to style. Lately, we’ve spotted these cozy kicks paired with everything from cozy joggers to shimmery dresses.

But with so many variations to choose from, it can be tough to find your new favorite pair. Fortunately, after trying out multiple slippers and gaining some insight from fellow editors, we’ve tracked down the top pairs. Keeping in mind comfort, design, and functionality, we uncovered the best Ugg slippers that are constantly coveted by your favorite celebrities (and us here at InStyle).

Best Overall

UGG Fluff Yeah Slide

What We Love: They come in an abundance of fun colors and patterns. 

What We Don’t Love: There’s a strap at the back, so you can’t just slide them on. 

Loved by celebrities like Megan Fox, Addison Rae, and Gigi Hadid (among many others), these plush slides have become an internet sensation, often selling out after their initial restock. Featuring a thick sole and a fluffy exterior, these slides exude a coziness so yummy you’ll never want to take them off. Plus, they come in over two dozen colors and patterns, from a bold checkerboard to classic black. At the back, an elastic strap secures your heel — although it does make the slipper a little trickier to put on. 

Wear these slippers while lounging at home, or take a note out of celebrities’ books and sport them with jeans, biker shorts, or leggings out on the street. This ultimate model-off-duty slide is the cool, head-turning basic you never knew you needed. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Sheepskin, rubber (outsole) | Colors: 27

Best Classic

UGG Coquette Slipper

UGG 

What We Love: The timeless design means these slippers will be in your closet for years to come. 

What We Don’t Love: The sheepskin insoles wear off at the heel over time. 

I’ve owned the Coquette Slipper for years and I refuse to move on — they’re just that good. You can’t go wrong with a classic slip-on, and this Ugg slide checks all the boxes: lightweight, comfortable, and easy to get on and off. Lined with fluffy sheepskin, these shoes can make even the coldest feet feel toasty warm. We also like that the foam outsole can keep up with you during any late-night ice cream runs or bodega trips. 

The timeless design will last for years in your shoe collection, but you may notice that the sheepskin insole may wear off at the heel over time. (Still, that’s standard for most fuzzy slippers.)

Size: 5–12 | Material: Sheepskin, EVA foam (outsole) | Colors: 4

Best Clog

UGG Fuzz Sugar Clog

UGG

What We Love: These clogs are made using Ugg’s SugarSole technology, which reduces the use of fossil fuels. 

What We Don’t Love: They aren’t durable enough to wear outdoors.

Embrace the “ugly” shoe style from the comfort of your own home with the Ugg Fuzz Sugar Clog. A fun take on the standard flat slip-on, these chunky clogs have a thick foam midsole and a rubber outsole for all-day comfort. Sleek and understated, they pair well with cozy work-from-home outfits or your go-to flared leggings. 

We also love that the Fuzz Sugar Clog is environmentally conscious — made from Tencel lyocell, a plush lining that comes from responsibly harvested trees, and recycled polyester, you can feel good about your purchase. Note: This style isn’t as durable as some of our other favorites are, so we wouldn’t recommend wearing them outdoors. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Tencel lyocell, recycled polyester, SugarSole EVA (midsole), recycled rubber (outsole) | Colors: 3

Best Platform

UGG Funkette Slipper

What We Love: The funky layered sole is perfect if you’re looking for something unique. 

What We Don’t Love: The elastic strap can loosen over time. 

The Funkette Slipper is the Coquette Slipper’s fun, retro aunt. Most notable for its thick, layered sole, this slingback shoe boasts a 2-inch platform heel and a luxe sheepskin lining for added warmth. But unlike some other Ugg slippers, you won’t overheat in these while walking your dog or grabbing coffee because they still leave your heels exposed.

The elastic backstrap keeps these slides firmly on your feet, although it may start to stretch out after repeated wear. Plus, the sturdy outsole can stand up to some serious outdoor mileage. With options ranging from solid black to hot pink, there’s a pair to match every minimalist or maximalist wardrobe. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Sheepskin, suede, EVA foam (midsole), SugarSole EVA (outsole) | Colors: 5

Best Moccasin

UGG Dakota Slipper

What We Love: The rubber outsole makes them easy to wear inside and out.

What We Don’t Love: They may feel tight at first, but as you wear them, the wool lining molds to your feet. 

Crafted in warm, upcycled wool and Tencel lyocell, the Dakota style hugs your feet with every step you take. It comes in five neutral colors with leather laces, a preppy addition we love to see. Pair these moccasins with your favorite sweats or take them out on the town — the water-resistant suede upper can handle an outing or two.

When you first slide the Dakota on, you may notice that the thick, fluffy wool feels a little tight. After some wear, the wool will start to mold to your feet, making them extra comfortable. In between sizes? Order a size up for the best fit. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Suede, leather, wool, Tencel lyocell, rubber (outsole) | Colors: 5

Best for Outside Wear

UGG Tazz

UGG

What We Love: This viral style is a celebrity favorite that goes with almost any outfit.

What We Don’t Love: Your heel may slip out of the back.

Elsa Hosk and Gigi Hadid aren’t the only fans of the Tazz Mule. These elevated (literally — they have a 1.75-inch platform) slippers have quickly become an it-girl must-have. We’ve seen them styled with everything from jeans to oversized blazers, making them great for those looking for something that’s easy to throw on. 

Designed for indoor-outdoor wear, the Tazz Mule is lined with Ugg’s signature UggPlush, a cozy, moisture-wicking blend of upcycled wool and Tencel lyocell. The chunky platform revamps Ugg’s classic slipper design and feels like you’re walking on a cloud. But it does dip lower at the back of the heel, meaning your feet may slip out from time to time. Be sure to use the size chart to find the right fit. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Recycled polyester, rayon, wool, Tencel lyocell, EVA foam (midsole and outsole) | Colors: 2

Best Slip-On

UGG Scuff Sis

What We Love: The fluffy collar adds a fun flare to basic slip-ons.

What We Don’t Love: The sole is thinner and less cushioned. 

Slippers are meant to be convenient: You want something you can effortlessly slide on and off. These no-fuss Scuff Sis Slippers do just that, with a fuzzy collar that instantly adds a luxurious feel. Available in basic Chestnut and bright shades like Pink Glow or Purple Ruby, there are plenty of options to choose from.

Ultra-comfortable suede and sheepskin combined with a thin sole make these slippers so lightweight that you’ll forget you’re wearing them. Pair them with a silk robe for all the drama. But if you’re after something with a sturdier bottom or more cushioning, we recommend opting for one of our other favorite pairs.

Size: 5–12 | Material: Sheepskin, suede, rubber (outsole) | Colors: 6

Most Stylish

UGG Scuffita Speckles Slipper

Ugg

What We Love: The fluffy cross straps and animal print stand out.

What We Don’t Love: They’re fairly flat, and they don’t have as much cushioning.

The Scuffita Spotty Slipper is uniquely architectural, with crossed sheepskin straps and a subtle animal print calf hair overlay that sets it apart from other Uggs on our list. We love the peep toe silhouette, which feels a little more elegant than a traditional moccasin. The luxe style instantly elevates basic loungewear. 

Inside, lush sheepskin stays soft to the touch as you strut from your bedroom to the couch. Plus, a thin rubber sole helps these slippers feel sturdier than your usual house shoes. However, it also means that the Scuffita Spotty isn’t as supportive as some of our other top picks are. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Sheepskin, calf hair, rubber (outsole) | Colors: 1

Best Slide

UGG Cozetta Curly Slides

UGG

What We Love: The deep treads on the soles are ideal for indoor-outdoor wear.

What We Don’t Love: They tend to run large, so we recommend ordering one size down from your usual.

Plush, curly sheepskin combines with upcycled wool and Tencel lyocell to create the ultimate cozy slide slipper. Designed with a little extra support and an outsole with deeper treads, the Cozetta Curly Slipper was made for indoor-outdoor wear. 

We love the sleek silhouette and upscale neutral shades — the warm gray Goat shade is one of our favorites. Or, try Pink Glow for a Barbie-inspired pop of color. An all-season staple, these will quickly become one of your go-to’s for running errands in style. Add a sleek pair of travel pants for an airport outfit that feels just like wearing your favorite pajamas. 

Size: 5-12 | Material: Sheepskin, wool, Tencel lyocell, EVA foam (outsole) | Colors: 7

Best Warm

UGG Disquette Slipper

UGG

What We Love: The classic Ugg shape and 1-inch platform make these easy to wear and style.

What We Don’t Love: They may feel heavier than some other pairs on our list do.

If your feet are always freezing, then the Disquette Slipper is a must-try. The fuzzy trim is a dead giveaway — these slippers are seriously warm (no socks needed). With a sheepskin collar, lining, and insole, the Disquette is packed with insulation to keep your toes feeling nice and toasty.

The 1-inch platform provides a little lift but is still easy to walk in, while the suede upper looks chic with everything from wide-leg jeans to structured slacks. However, they are on the chunkier side and may feel heavier than some of our other top picks, like the Scuffita Speckles Slipper. 

Size: 5–12 | Material: Suede, sheepskin, EVA foam (midsole and outsole) | Colors: 8

Best for Wide Feet

UGG Ansley Slippers

Nordstrom

What We Love: The sleek silhouette comes in a wide range of bright colors.

What We Don’t Love: The flat footbed isn’t as supportive as some others on our list.

For a fresh take on the Dakota Slipper that’s also wide-feet-friendly, we recommend the Ansley Slipper. This moccasin comes in two widths and has a wider heel — so if you’re tired of fleecy Ugg slippers always feeling too tight, this style was made for you.

The Ansley has a modern silhouette and a molded rubber outsole that’s ideal for curling up on the couch or running to the drugstore. We also like that the suede upper is water-resistant, to keep them looking like new longer. Decorative stitching around the sides and heel adds a subtle flair (similar to the Dakota’s leather laces). Plus, they’re so comfortable that you may need more than one pair — don’t say we didn’t warn you.

Size: 5–12 | Material: Suede, Tencel lyocell, wool, rubber | Colors: 13

What To Keep In Mind

Color

Most Ugg slippers come in a wide range of bright colors and fun patterns. If you’re looking to expand beyond basic black or classic Chestnut, there are plenty of options. Try the Cozetta Curly in Pink Glow or rock the Fluff Yeah Slide in Key Lime. We also love Ugg’s upscale neutrals — the Disquette Slipper comes in Goose, a stunning steel blue. Ultimately, the best color slipper is one that matches your wardrobe and makes you happy every time you wear it. 

Heel Height

When considering heel height for slippers, think about where you plan to wear them. If you’re looking for an indoor-outdoor slipper, you’re going to want something with a taller, more durable sole, like the Tazz Slipper. But if you’re just going to wear them around your home while lounging, you can get away with a pair that has a thinner sole, like the Scuff Sis.

Slide vs. Strap

Ugg offers both slippers with a strap and standard slides. Slides are easier to slip on and off since you don’t have to fuss with any backstrap or closure. But pairs with elastic straps (like the Fluff Yeah Slide) are easier to wear outside without the fear of your feet slipping out and onto the pavement.

Your Questions, Answered

How do you clean Ugg slippers?

If you want your Uggs to last for years to come, you may want to pre-treat them with a water- and stain-resistant spray. But when life happens, rest assured — there are easy ways to clean your slippers. First, refresh your shoes by brushing them with a clean, damp sponge. (Just make sure you stuff the inside of your slippers with paper towels and allow them to dry completely once you’re done.) Then, if you have a suede cleaner, you can use that on the slippers’ exterior. Sound overwhelming? Ugg’s Care Kit includes everything you need to keep your slippers looking (and smelling) great.

Do Ugg slippers stretch out over time?

Naturally, sheepskin does tend to stretch out slightly over time. You’ll discover that the more you wear your Uggs, the more the wool and sheepskin will start to mold to the shape of your feet.

Do Ugg slippers run big or small?

Ugg recommends sizing down when purchasing its sheepskin slippers. Over time, the shoes will give a bit and mold to your feet.
