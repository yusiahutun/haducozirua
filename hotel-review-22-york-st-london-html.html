Hotel review: 22 York St, London

 Posted on June 29, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  425 words  |  Larita Shotwell

22 York Street: Probably not the finest hotel in London

This B&B near Baker Street Underground station should be a lot cheaper than it is. A double costs £120 and a single £89, and I'm really not sure how it gets away with such prices.

Yes, this is Central London, a mere hop and tourist skip from Madame Tussauds and, yes, it occupies a grand Georgian town house. But it still falls short on several fronts despite a glowing write-up (paid for, of course) in one of the more reputable B&B guide books.

'A very friendly place,' I had read. 'And there's always something to catch your eye.'

Well, it wasn't unfriendly when I arrived on a Monday evening, just rather soulless and strangely dispiriting. This might have had something to do with the owners, Michael and Liz Callis, not being in residence. Apparently, they were in France, where they run a small hotel, and I was to discover later that they have just opened a third venture in Switzerland.

It could also have been because I was almost the only person to have booked a room, which might just explain why I received a call earlier in the day asking me what time I would arrive, with a request that it should not be after 9pm. Frankly, if I'm paying £89 for a tiny bed in a room with a dirty rug and naff net curtains, then I think I should be allowed to arrive whenever I want.

Estate agents tend to call a house a 'little tired' when they know it's falling apart. My room was very tired indeed, and hideous in every possible respect.

The ottoman at the foot of the bed was nearly as grubby as the rug lying beside it and the bathroom reminded me of a boarding house horror that might appear in a Mike Leigh film. All would be well in the morning - but the morning took a long time to come because the window rattled like a freight train.

Even so, I arrived downstairs with expectations high for a cheering feast - and didn't get one. In fact, I was horrified to learn that 22 York Street doesn't do cooked breakfast. Instead, you help yourself to cereal from big plastic containers and there's a bowl of bananas on the side. What's more, it was just me. Either the other guests had vanished, or there weren't any.
