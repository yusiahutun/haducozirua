About Gardner

Jim Gardner was born as James Goldman on May 17, 1948, in New York City, New York, United States. Gardner holds an American nationality and belongs to white ethnicity. The details of his early life, parents, and siblings are not available.

As far as his education is concerned, Gardner graduated from Columbia University with a Bachelor of Arts in political science in 1970. Later, he attended Brown University in order to earn a Master’s Degree.

Career Overview

Jim first reported on the Historic Student Riots at Columbia in 1968 for the university’s radio station, WKCR-FM. He became a desk assistant, writer, and producer for 1010 WINS in New York City in 1970. Likewise, he also served as a reporter for WFAS radio in White Plains, New York and soon he became News Director. Gardner began his television broadcasting career at WKBW-TV in Buffalo, New York.

Since June 1, 1976, he is working for WPVI-TV. Currently, Jim solo anchors the 6:00 PM and 11:00 PM weekday newscast, which he began doing on May 11, 1977. Apart, he also covered every Democratic and Republican presidential convention.
